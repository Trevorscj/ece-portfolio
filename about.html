Biography & Engineering Philosophy

I'm currently an undergrad at Georgia Tech pursuing a B.S. in Electrical Engineering with a Minor in Quantum Sciences and Technology. I'm genuinely obsessed with the baseline physics that make solid-state hardware and modern microelectronics tick. For me, it's not enough to know that a circuit works; I want to understand exactly why it was designed that way in the first place.

By prioritizing that kind of intuitive understanding, I've found it much easier to troubleshoot edge cases and track down weird architectural bugs. More importantly, it makes me a highly adaptable teammate when it comes time to integrate into fast-paced engineering teams building complex, commercially viable hardware.

Technical Interests: Deep Space Astrophotography

Outside of cleanrooms and robotic testbeds, my biggest technical hobby is deep-space astrophotography. Capturing distant galaxies and nebulae means dealing with some incredibly tight physical and optical tolerances. You have to nail your polar alignment, perfectly track the Earth's rotation, and carefully calibrate sensors to get a clean signal-to-noise ratio out of minimal light exposures.

Honestly, it's a lot like my engineering coursework. Managing hardware failures in the field, tracking thermal noise on imaging sensors, and compiling multi-spectral datasets has done wonders for my patience and problem-solving skills.
