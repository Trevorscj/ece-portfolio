Biography & Engineering Philosophy

My academic approach values rigorous self-discovery and independent research, working to understand the structural limitations and baseline physics of modern microelectronics components.

Technical Interests: Deep Space Astrophotography

Outside of cleanrooms and robotic testbeds, my primary technical interest is deep-space astrophotography. Capturing distant celestial structures requires balancing tight tolerances across physical, optical, and computational systems. Imaging nebulae and distant galaxies demands precise polar alignment, fine tracking control to counter earth's rotation, and meticulous sensor calibration to optimize signal-to-noise ratios under minimal exposure conditions.

This pursuit directly exercises system optimization skills that mirror my engineering coursework. Managing hardware failures in the field, tracking thermal noise profiles on imaging sensors, and compiling multi-spectral datasets has significantly sharpened my patience and systematic problem-solving methods.
